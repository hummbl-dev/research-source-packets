# v0.1 Scope and Boundary

This document defines the scope, boundary, and review expectations for the
**v0.1** release of the research-source-packets candidate namespace. It is
exploratory and non-canon until reviewed and adopted through a governance path.

## Scope

v0.1 defines the smallest useful, reviewable shape of a research source packet:

- A versioned, machine-readable packet manifest.
- A source contract describing the source being represented: type, citation,
  provenance, evidence grade, and verification status.
- Receipt requirements describing what a consuming agent or reviewer must
  record when accepting, rejecting, or citing a packet.
- A minimal JSON schema, one worked example, and valid/invalid fixtures.
- Prior art and adjacent ecosystem references.

v0.1 is intentionally narrow. It does not define a full citation graph,
evidence-chain execution engine, or agent runtime. It defines the packet
artifact and the contract around it.

## Non-goals

v0.1 does **not** attempt to:

- Define a universal standard for research source representation.
- Claim ownership of the field, terminology, or any adjacent namespace.
- Canonize HUMMBL, BaseN, Ownward, or any private/internal concepts. Any such
  references must be explicitly marked as candidate and audited separately.
- Specify a transport, storage, or execution layer.
- Replace existing reference-management or citation-graph systems.
- Include private, internal, or secret operational content.

## Adoption boundary

- v0.1 is a **candidate** release. Nothing here is adopted guidance.
- Adoption requires a reviewed governance path that is not yet defined.
- Consumers may experiment with v0.1 packets, but must not treat them as
  authoritative or stable.
- Packet consumers must treat `schemaVersion` as the only compatibility
  boundary. Future versions may break shape without notice while v0.x.

## Review expectations

Reviewers of v0.1 artifacts should check for:

- Provenance is present and traceable to a public source where possible.
- Evidence grade is stated, not implied.
- Candidate status is marked explicitly; nothing is silently canonized.
- No private, internal, or secret operational content is present.
- The packet is small, reviewable, and self-describing.

## Non-canon posture

This repository is a public seed. All v0.1 content is **non-canon** unless and
until it is adopted through a reviewed governance path. The maintainers make no
claim of authority over the broader field or its terminology.

## Required packet pieces

A v0.1 research source packet must contain, at minimum:

1. `schemaVersion` — the packet schema version (`v0.1`).
2. `packetStatus` — lifecycle status of the packet (e.g. `candidate`).
3. `packetManifest` — `id`, `title`, `version`, and `scope` of the packet.
4. `authority` — who produced the packet and under what posture.
5. `sourceContract` — the source being represented:
   - `sourceType`
   - `citation`
   - `provenance`
   - `evidenceGrade`
   - `verificationStatus`
6. `receiptRequirements` — what a consumer must record when accepting the
   packet.

Packets missing any required piece are invalid under v0.1.
